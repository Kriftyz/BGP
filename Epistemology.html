Introduction to Epistemology

What does it mean to know something? You might be certain that the sky is blue, that 2 + 2 = 4, or that you’re reading this right now—but how do you know for sure? Could you be mistaken? Could your senses be deceiving you? Epistemology is the branch of philosophy that investigates knowledge, belief, and truth. It explores how we form beliefs, what it means to be justified in holding those beliefs, and whether we can ever have absolute certainty about anything. These questions aren’t just abstract—they shape the way we think about science, history, law, and even our everyday decisions. In this section, we’ll explore the fundamental ideas of epistemology, from ancient skepticism to modern theories of truth and knowledge.
